## 2. Demise and Term

2.1 The Landlord demises the Premises to the Tenant for the Term, subject to the provisions of this Lease.

2.2 The Tenant pays the Rent, the Insurance Rent, the Service Charge and all other sums payable under this Lease at the times and in the manner provided.

2.3 This Lease is an FRI (Full Repairing and Insuring) lease. The Tenant shall bear all costs associated with the repair, maintenance and insurance of the Premises.

---

## 3. Rent

3.1 The Tenant shall pay the Rent by equal quarterly instalments in advance on the usual quarter days (25 March, 24 June, 29 September and 25 December) or on [[RENT_PAYMENT_DAYS]].

3.2 The first payment of Rent shall be a proportionate payment from the Rent Commencement Date to the next quarter day, calculated on a daily basis.

3.3 The Tenant shall pay the Rent without any deduction or set-off except as required by law or expressly permitted by this Lease.

3.4 If the Rent or any other sum due under this Lease is unpaid for [[RENT_GRACE_PERIOD_DAYS]] days after the due date, the Tenant shall pay interest on the outstanding amount at the Interest Rate from the due date until payment.

3.5 The Landlord may serve a notice under section 17 of the Landlord and Tenant (Covenants) Act 1995 in respect of any fixed charge (as defined therein) payable under this Lease.

---

## 4. Rent Review

4.1 The Rent shall be reviewed on each Rent Review Date.

4.2 The reviewed Rent shall be the open market rent at the relevant Rent Review Date for a letting of the Premises on the same terms as this Lease (but disregarding the fact that the Tenant is in occupation), assuming:
   (a) the Premises are available for letting on the open market without a tenant;
   (b) the Premises are in good repair and condition as required by this Lease;
   (c) the Permitted Use is the highest value use authorised under this Lease;
   (d) any alterations, improvements or fit-out carried out by the Tenant with Consent are disregarded (unless the Tenant is obliged to remove them or has received a rental concession for them).

4.3 If the reviewed Rent cannot be agreed within [[RENT_REVIEW_NEGOTIATION_PERIOD]] days, either party may refer the matter to an independent surveyor (appointed by the President of the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors) whose decision shall be binding.

4.4 The reviewed Rent shall take effect from the Rent Review Date.

4.5 There shall be [[NUMBER_OF_RENT_FREEZE_PERIODS]] rent-free periods of [[RENT_FREEZE_DURATION]] months in respect of any step-change in Rent on review.

## 5. Service Charge

5.1 The Tenant shall pay the Service Charge to the Landlord [[SERVICE_CHARGE_PAYMENT_FREQUENCY]].

5.2 The Service Charge shall be calculated by the Landlord's Surveyor and certified annually. The Tenant may, within [[SERVICE_CHARGE_CHALLENGE_PERIOD]] days of receiving the certificate, challenge the Service Charge by written notice to the Landlord's Surveyor.

5.3 The Service Charge shall include the costs of:
   (a) heating, lighting, cleaning and maintaining the Common Parts;
   (b) providing security for the Building;
   (c) maintaining and repairing the structure and exterior of the Building (to the extent not covered by insurance);
   (d) managing the Building;
   (e) a management fee of [[MANAGEMENT_FEE_PERCENTAGE]]% of the total Service Charge;
   (f) a sinking fund contribution of [[SINKING_FUND_CONTRIBUTION]] per annum;
   (g) such other services as the Landlord reasonably determines.

## 6. Repairs and Insurance

6.1 **Repairing Obligations**

   (a) The Tenant shall keep the Premises in good and substantial repair and condition throughout the Term and yield them up in such condition at the Termination Date.

   (b) The Tenant shall keep the interior of the Premises (including ceilings, floors, windows, doors, skirting, sanitary fittings, pipes, cables, conduits and all fixtures and fittings) in good repair and condition.

   (c) The Tenant shall not remove any of the Landlord's fixtures and fittings.

   (d) The Tenant shall comply with all statutory requirements relating to the Premises and the Tenant's use of them.

   (e) The Tenant shall keep the exterior of the Premises clean and in good decorative order, repainting or redecorating as reasonably required (at least every [[REPAINT_CYCLE_YEARS]] years).

6.2 **Schedule of Condition**

   (a) The parties have either (i) agreed a photographic and written Schedule of Condition annexed to this Lease; or (ii) elected not to prepare a Schedule of Condition.

   (b) If a Schedule of Condition exists, the Tenant's repairing obligation shall be limited to returning the Premises in no worse condition than that recorded in the Schedule (fair wear and tear excepted).

6.3 **Insurance**

   (a) The Landlord shall insure the Premises and the Building against the Insured Risks for their full reinstatement value and shall maintain such other insurance as the Landlord reasonably considers appropriate.

   (b) The Tenant shall pay the Insurance Rent within [[INSURANCE_PAYMENT_DAYS]] days of demand.

   (c) The Tenant shall not do anything that would invalidate the insurance policies or increase the premium.

   (d) If the Premises are damaged by an Insured Risk, the Landlord shall reinstate them as soon as reasonably practicable, and the Rent shall abate proportionately until reinstatement is complete.

   (e) If the Premises are damaged by an uninsured risk, the Tenant shall reinstate them at the Tenant's own cost.

## 7. Alterations

7.1 The Tenant shall not make any structural alterations to the Premises without the Landlord's Consent.

7.2 The Tenant may make non-structural alterations (including partitioning, internal redecoration and installation of telecommunications wiring) without Consent, provided that:
   (a) the Tenant gives the Landlord not less than [[ALTERATION_NOTICE_DAYS]] days' prior written notice;
   (b) the alterations do not affect the structure or services of the Building;
   (c) the Tenant complies with all statutory requirements and planning laws;
   (d) the Tenant reinstates the Premises at the end of the Term if the Landlord so requires.

7.3 The Landlord may impose reasonable conditions on any Consent, including requiring the Tenant to reinstate the Premises at the end of the Term.

7.4 The Tenant shall pay the Landlord's reasonable legal and surveyor's costs in connection with any application for Consent.

---

## 8. Assignment and Subletting

8.1 The Tenant shall not assign, sublet, charge or part with possession of the whole or any part of the Premises without the Landlord's Consent.

8.2 The Landlord shall not unreasonably withhold Consent to an assignment of the whole of the Premises to a permitted assignee, provided that:
   (a) the proposed assignee has a net worth of at least [[ASSIGNEE_MINIMUM_NET_WORTH]] or provides a guarantor or rent deposit acceptable to the Landlord;
   (b) the proposed assignee carries on a business within the Permitted Use or a use that the Landlord (acting reasonably) considers appropriate;
   (c) the Tenant provides an authorised guarantee agreement (AGA) if required under the Landlord and Tenant (Covenants) Act 1995;
   (d) there is no subsisting breach of covenant by the Tenant;
   (e) the Tenant pays the Landlord's reasonable legal and surveyor's costs.

8.3 Any subletting shall be on terms consistent with this Lease and at a rent not less than the open market rent.

8.4 No assignment or subletting shall be to any person with whom the Landlord has an environmental, reputational or other reasonable objection.

## 9. Break Clause

9.1 The Tenant may terminate this Lease on the Break Date by giving not less than the Break Notice Period written notice (a "Break Notice") to the Landlord.

9.2 The Break Notice must be served not earlier than [[BREAK_EARLIEST_NOTICE_DATE]] and not later than [[BREAK_LATEST_NOTICE_DATE]].

9.3 The break right is conditional upon:
   (a) there being no subsisting breach of any obligation under this Lease at the date of the Break Notice or the Break Date;
   (b) the Tenant giving vacant possession of the Premises on the Break Date;
   (c) the Tenant paying the Break Amount (if any) on or before the Break Date;
   (d) the Tenant paying all sums due under this Lease up to and including the Break Date.

9.4 There shall be no payment of Rent or other sums after the Break Date.

9.5 The Landlord may also terminate this Lease on the Break Date by giving not less than the Break Notice Period written notice (a "Landlord's Break Notice"), subject to the same conditions.

---

## 10. Forfeiture

10.1 The Landlord may re-enter the Premises (or any part of them) and forfeit this Lease if:
   (a) the Rent or any part of it is unpaid for [[FORFEITURE_RENT_ARREARS_DAYS]] days after becoming due (whether formally demanded or not);
   (b) the Tenant breaches any covenant or obligation under this Lease and fails to remedy it within [[FORFEITURE_CURE_PERIOD]] days of written notice;
   (c) the Tenant becomes insolvent, enters administration, has a receiver appointed, passes a resolution for winding up, or enters into any composition with creditors under the Insolvency Act 1986;
   (d) the Tenant ceases to occupy the Premises for a continuous period of [[NON_OCCUPATION_PERIOD]] months;
   (e) the Tenant is convicted of a criminal offence relating to the Premises or the permitted use.

10.2 The Tenant shall pay the Landlord's costs and expenses incurred in connection with any forfeiture proceedings.

10.3 Upon re-entry, this Lease shall determine absolutely, but without prejudice to any right of action of the Landlord in respect of any antecedent breach.

## 11. MEES / EPC Compliance

11.1 The Landlord warrants that on the Term Commencement Date the Premises have a valid EPC with a rating of at least [[EPC_MINIMUM_RATING]].

11.2 The Tenant shall:
   (a) not carry out any alterations or changes of use that would adversely affect the EPC rating;
   (b) provide the Landlord with all information reasonably required to maintain or obtain an EPC;
   (c) comply with the Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards (MEES) and not cause the Premises to fall below the minimum required EPC rating.

11.3 The Landlord may carry out energy efficiency improvement works at any time (subject to reasonable access) and may pass the cost through the Service Charge or as a direct charge, provided that:
   (a) the works are reasonably necessary to achieve or maintain MEES compliance;
   (b) the cost is proportionate and represents value for money;
   (c) the works do not unreasonably interfere with the Tenant's use of the Premises.

11.4 If the EPC rating falls below the minimum standard due to the Tenant's acts or omissions, the Tenant shall indemnify the Landlord against all losses, costs, claims and expenses arising from such non-compliance.

11.5 The parties shall cooperate to obtain a valid EPC and provide access for EPC assessors as reasonably required.
