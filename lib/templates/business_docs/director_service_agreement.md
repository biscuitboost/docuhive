## 3. Duties (Statutory and Fiduciary)

3.1 The Director owes the statutory duties set out in sections 171 to 177 of the Act, which are codified as follows:

   (a) **s.171 — Duty to act within powers:** The Director must act in accordance with the Company's constitution and only exercise powers for the purposes for which they are conferred.

   (b) **s.172 — Duty to promote the success of the Company:** The Director must act in the way they consider, in good faith, would be most likely to promote the success of the Company for the benefit of its members as a whole, having regard to:
      - (i) the likely consequences of any decision in the long term;
      - (ii) the interests of the Company's employees;
      - (iii) the need to foster the Company's business relationships with suppliers, customers and others;
      - (iv) the impact of the Company's operations on the community and the environment;
      - (v) the desirability of the Company maintaining a reputation for high standards of business conduct; and
      - (vi) the need to act fairly as between members of the Company.

   (c) **s.173 — Duty to exercise independent judgment:** The Director must exercise independent judgment, subject to any agreement entered into by the Company or the Director that restricts the exercise of independent judgment.

   (d) **s.174 — Duty to exercise reasonable care, skill and diligence:** The Director must exercise the care, skill and diligence that would be exercised by a reasonably diligent person with both:
      - (i) the general knowledge, skill and experience that may reasonably be expected of a person carrying out the functions of the Director; and
      - (ii) the general knowledge, skill and experience that the Director actually has.

   (e) **s.175 — Duty to avoid conflicts of interest:** The Director must avoid a situation in which they have, or can have, a direct or indirect interest that conflicts, or possibly may conflict, with the interests of the Company. This duty applies in particular to the exploitation of any property, information or opportunity (regardless of whether the Company could itself take advantage of it).

   (f) **s.176 — Duty not to accept benefits from third parties:** The Director must not accept a benefit from a third party conferred by reason of being a director or doing (or not doing) anything as a director.

   (g) **s.177 — Duty to declare interest in proposed transaction or arrangement:** If the Director is in any way, directly or indirectly, interested in a proposed transaction or arrangement with the Company, the Director must declare the nature and extent of that interest to the other directors.

3.2 The Director shall:
   (a) exercise the powers, duties and functions conferred on them as a director;
   (b) perform such executive duties as the Board may from time to time reasonably assign;
   (c) attend Board meetings and committee meetings as reasonably required;
   (d) comply with the Company's policies and procedures (as amended from time to time);
   (e) devote sufficient time and attention to the Business to ensure the proper performance of their duties;
   (f) disclose to the Board any actual or potential conflict of interest immediately upon becoming aware of it;
   (g) not, without the prior written consent of the Board, accept any office, employment or engagement with any other entity that could reasonably be expected to conflict with the Director's duties.

## 6. Termination

6.1 **Notice:**
   (a) Either party may terminate the Director's employment and appointment under this Agreement by giving the Notice Period written notice to the other.
   (b) The Company may terminate the Director's employment and appointment with immediate effect by written notice if the Director is guilty of Gross Misconduct.
   (c) The Director's appointment as a director shall automatically terminate upon the termination of their employment under this Agreement.

6.2 **Payment in Lieu of Notice (PILON):**
   (a) The Company may, at its sole discretion, terminate the Director's employment with immediate effect by making a payment in lieu of notice equal to basic Salary (excluding benefits, bonus and pension contributions) for the Notice Period.
   (b) The Company may deduct from the PILON any sums owed by the Director to the Company.

6.3 **Termination on Death or Incapacity:**
   (a) The Director's employment shall automatically terminate on the date of the Director's death.
   (b) The Company may terminate the Director's employment if the Director is unable to perform their duties for a continuous period of [[LONG_TERM_SICKNESS_PERIOD]] months due to incapacity by giving written notice.

6.4 **Termination on Winding Up or Disqualification:**
   (a) The Director's appointment shall automatically terminate if the Director is disqualified from acting as a director under the Company Directors Disqualification Act 1986 or any equivalent legislation.
   (b) The Company may terminate the Director's employment if the Company enters administration, liquidation or a winding-up process.

6.5 **On Termination:**
   (a) The Director shall resign all directorships and other offices held in the Company and any Group company.
   (b) The Director shall return all Company property (including documents, devices, keys, credit cards and confidential information).
   (c) The Company shall make any payments due under this Agreement and under statute (including statutory redundancy pay if applicable).
   (d) The Director shall not be entitled to any bonus in respect of the financial year in which termination occurs, unless the Board otherwise determines.

## 7. Restrictive Covenants

7.1 The Director covenants that during the Restricted Period they shall not, without the prior written consent of the Board:

   (a) **Non-compete:** Be employed, engaged or interested in any business within the United Kingdom that is in competition with the Business (being [[COMPETITOR_DEFINITION]]);

   (b) **Non-solicitation of customers:** Solicit or deal with any person who was a customer or client of the Company during the [[NON_SOLICITATION_LOOKBACK]] months preceding the Termination Date and with whom the Director had material dealings;

   (c) **Non-solicitation of employees:** Induce or attempt to induce any senior employee, director or consultant of the Company to leave their employment or engagement;

   (d) **Non- dealing with suppliers:** Interfere with or seek to terminate any supply or service contract of the Company with any supplier with whom the Director had material dealings.

7.2 Each covenant in clause 7.1 is a separate and independent restriction. If any restriction is found to be void or unenforceable, it may be severed and the remaining restrictions shall continue in full force.

7.3 The Director acknowledges that the restrictions in this clause 7 are reasonable and necessary to protect the legitimate business interests of the Company.
