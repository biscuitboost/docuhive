   (a) The Board shall consider the declaration of dividends annually within [[DIVIDEND_CONSIDERATION_MONTHS]] months after the end of each financial year.

   (b) Dividends may be declared and paid out of distributable profits in accordance with Part 23 of the Act and the Articles.

## 6. Pre-Emption Rights

6.1 If the Company proposes to issue any New Securities, it shall first offer them to the existing Shareholders in proportion to their Shareholdings (the "Pre-Emption Rights").

6.2 The Company shall give each Shareholder a written notice (a "Pre-Emption Offer") specifying:
   (a) the number of New Securities to be issued;
   (b) the issue price per New Security (which shall be the Market Value or such other price as the Board determines in good faith);
   (c) the timetable for acceptance (not less than [[PRE_EMPTION_ACCEPTANCE_DAYS]] Business Days).

6.3 A Shareholder may accept the Pre-Emption Offer in whole or in part by giving written notice to the Company within the acceptance period.

6.4 New Securities not taken up by Shareholders may be offered to third parties on the same terms (or on terms not more favourable to the subscribers) within [[PRE_EMPTION_THIRD_PARTY_DAYS]] days after the acceptance deadline.

6.5 The provisions of this clause 6 shall not apply to:
   (a) the issue of Shares pursuant to an employee share scheme approved by the Board;
   (b) the issue of Shares in connection with an Exit;
   (c) the issue of Shares to a bank or financial institution as part of a debt restructuring.

## 7. Share Transfers — Right of First Refusal (ROFR)

7.1 A Shareholder wishing to Transfer any Shares (a "Transferor") must first comply with this clause 7.

7.2 The Transferor shall give a Transfer Notice to the Company specifying:
   (a) the number of Shares proposed to be transferred (the "Sale Shares");
   (b) the price per Share (the "Offer Price");
   (c) the identity of the proposed transferee (if any).

7.3 The Company shall within [[ROFR_NOTIFICATION_DAYS]] Business Days notify all other Shareholders (the "Remaining Shareholders") of the Transfer Notice.

7.4 Each Remaining Shareholder may elect to purchase all or a proportion of the Sale Shares at the Offer Price within [[ROFR_ACCEPTANCE_DAYS]] Business Days of the Company's notification.

7.5 If the Remaining Shareholders collectively elect to purchase all the Sale Shares, the Transferor shall sell them accordingly (pro rata to existing Shareholdings unless otherwise agreed).

7.6 If the Remaining Shareholders elect to purchase less than all the Sale Shares, the Transferor may Transfer the unpurchased Shares to a third party on the same terms as the Transfer Notice within [[ROFR_THIRD_PARTY_DAYS]] days, provided that:
   (a) the third party executes a deed of adherence agreeing to be bound by this Agreement;
   (b) the Transfer price is not less than the Offer Price.

7.7 If the Transferor does not complete the Transfer within the period specified in clause 7.6, the ROFR procedure must be repeated before any subsequent transfer.

7.8 The provisions of this clause 7 shall not apply to transfers of Shares to a Shareholder's family trust, spouse, civil partner, or direct descendants (a "Permitted Transfer"), provided the transferee executes a deed of adherence.

## 8. Drag Along and Tag Along Rights

8.1 **Tag Along (Co-Sale) Rights**

   (a) If a Shareholder (a "Selling Shareholder") proposes to Transfer Shares to a third party (a "Third Party Purchaser") and such Transfer would result in the Third Party Purchaser holding more than [[TAG_ALONG_THRESHOLD]]% of the Shares, each other Shareholder (a "Tagging Shareholder") shall have the right to Tag Along.

   (b) The Selling Shareholder shall give each Tagging Shareholder a Tag Along Notice specifying the proposed terms of the Transfer.

   (c) Each Tagging Shareholder may elect to sell all or a proportion of their Shares on the same terms and conditions as the Selling Shareholder (including price).

   (d) The Third Party Purchaser shall purchase the Tagging Shareholders' Shares on the same terms.

8.2 The Tag Along Notice period shall be [[TAG_ALONG_ACCEPTANCE_DAYS]] Business Days.

8.3 **Drag Along Rights**

   (a) If Shareholders holding at least [[DRAG_ALONG_THRESHOLD]]% of the Shares (the "Dragging Shareholders") propose to accept a bona fide third-party offer to acquire all the Shares of the Company, the Dragging Shareholders may require the other Shareholders (the "Dragged Shareholders") to Transfer all their Shares on the same terms (a "Drag Along Sale").

   (b) The Dragging Shareholders shall give each Dragged Shareholder a Drag Along Notice specifying:
      - (i) the identity of the third-party purchaser;
      - (ii) the price per Share;
      - (iii) the other material terms of the proposed sale;
      - (iv) the deadline for completion (not less than [[DRAG_ALONG_COMPLETION_DAYS]] Business Days from the notice).

   (c) Each Dragged Shareholder shall be obliged to Transfer their Shares on the same terms and shall:
      - (i) give all necessary warranties, representations and indemnities on a several basis (limited to their pro rata share of any claim);
      - (ii) give all necessary consents and approvals;
      - (iii) execute all documents required to effect the Drag Along Sale.

   (d) No Dragged Shareholder shall be required to give warranties greater than those given by the Dragging Shareholders or to give any warranty in respect of matters relating to any period before they became a Shareholder.

8.4 Any Shareholder who fails to comply with their obligations under a Drag Along Sale shall indemnify the Dragging Shareholders against all losses and costs arising from such failure.

## 9. Good Leaver / Bad Leaver Provisions

9.1 **Good Leaver**

   A Shareholder who is also a director or employee of the Company shall be treated as a "Good Leaver" if they cease to be a director or employee due to:
   (a) death;
   (b) ill health, injury or disability (certified by a medical practitioner);
   (c) redundancy;
   (d) retirement at or after normal pension age;
   (e) the Company terminating their employment or directorship otherwise than for cause;
   (f) such other circumstances as the Board (including the Investor Majority) determines in its absolute discretion.

9.2 **Bad Leaver**

   A Shareholder who is also a director or employee shall be treated as a "Bad Leaver" if they cease to be a director or employee:
   (a) by resignation (other than for Good Leaver reasons);
   (b) by termination for cause (including gross misconduct, fraud, dishonesty, material breach of duty);
   (c) by termination without cause where the Board (including the Investor Majority) determines to treat them as a Bad Leaver.

9.3 **Transfer of Leaver Shares**

   (a) A Leaver shall be obliged to Transfer all their Shares (the "Leaver Shares") within [[LEAVER_TRANSFER_DAYS]] days of the Leaver Event.

   (b) **Good Leaver Shares:** The purchase price per Share shall be the higher of (i) Market Value; and (ii) the price paid by the Leaver for the Shares (or, if the Shares were subscribed, the subscription price).

   (c) **Bad Leaver Shares:** The purchase price per Share shall be the lower of (i) Market Value; and (ii) [[BAD_LEAVER_PRICE]] (or, if lower, the par value).

9.4 The purchasing Shareholders (and/or the Company, subject to the Act and the Articles) shall purchase the Leaver Shares pro rata to their existing Shareholdings within [[LEAVER_PURCHASE_DAYS]] days.

9.5 Payment for Leaver Shares shall be made in cash within [[LEAVER_PAYMENT_DAYS]] days of completion of the Transfer.

## 10. Deadlock Resolution

10.1 A "Deadlock" shall be deemed to occur if:
   (a) the Board has met twice to consider a Reserved Matter and has been unable to reach a decision for [[DEADLOCK_PERIOD_DAYS]] consecutive days; or
   (b) any party holding [[DEADLOCK_BLOCKING_PERCENTAGE]]% or more of the voting rights certifies in writing that a deadlock exists in relation to a fundamental matter of the Business.

10.2 **Deadlock Resolution Procedure**

   Stage 1 — **Negotiation:** Within [[DEADLOCK_NEGOTIATION_DAYS]] days of a Deadlock being declared, the Shareholders shall meet and attempt in good faith to resolve the Deadlock.

   Stage 2 — **Mediation:** If the Deadlock is not resolved within Stage 1, the Shareholders shall refer the matter to the Centre for Effective Dispute Resolution (CEDR) or [[MEDIATION_BODY]] for mediation. The costs of mediation shall be shared equally.

   Stage 3 — **Russian Roulette / Texas Shoot-Out:** If mediation is unsuccessful within [[MEDIATION_PERIOD_DAYS]] days, either Shareholder group may serve a notice (a "Buy-Out Notice") offering to purchase all the Shares of the other Shareholder group at a price per Share specified in the notice (the "Offer Price").

   (a) The receiving Shareholder group may within [[SHOOT_OUT_ACCEPTANCE_DAYS]] days either:
      - (i) accept the offer and sell its Shares at the Offer Price; or
      - (ii) purchase the offering Shareholder group's Shares at the Offer Price.

   (b) If the receiving group does not respond within the acceptance period, it shall be deemed to have accepted the offer.

   (c) Completion of the buy-out shall occur within [[SHOOT_OUT_COMPLETION_DAYS]] days of acceptance.

10.3 If the Deadlock cannot be resolved through the procedure above, either Shareholder group may petition the court for the winding up of the Company on just and equitable grounds under section 122(1)(g) of the Insolvency Act 1986.

## 11. Non-Compete

11.1 Each Shareholder who is also a director or employee of the Company covenants that during the Non-Compete Period they shall not, without the prior written consent of the Board (including the Investor Majority):
   (a) directly or indirectly carry on, be engaged, concerned or interested in any business that is in competition with the Business within the United Kingdom;
   (b) solicit or deal with any customer or client of the Company with whom they had material dealings during the [[NON_COMPETE_LOOKBACK]] months preceding the termination of their association with the Company;
   (c) solicit or induce any employee, director or consultant of the Company to leave their employment or engagement.

11.2 Each covenant in clause 11.1 is a separate and independent restriction. If any restriction is found to be void or unenforceable, it may be severed.

11.3 The Shareholder acknowledges that the restrictions are reasonable and necessary to protect the legitimate business interests of the Company.
