## 2. Background

(A) The Controller is a data controller within the meaning of the **UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)** and the **Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018)** as amended by the **Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 (DUAA 2025)**.

(B) The Processor provides certain services to the Controller as more particularly described in the **Service Agreement** dated [[SERVICE_AGREEMENT_DATE]] (the "**Service Agreement**") between the Controller and the Processor.

(C) In providing the Services, the Processor may process personal data on behalf of the Controller. The Parties intend that this Data Processing Agreement (the "**Agreement**") shall govern such processing and shall comply with Article 28 UK GDPR, DPA 2018, and DUAA 2025.

(D) This Agreement forms part of the Service Agreement. In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and the Service Agreement, this Agreement shall prevail in respect of data protection matters.

The Processor shall process personal data on behalf of the Controller only for the purposes described in Schedule 1 to this Agreement and in accordance with the documented instructions of the Controller (the "**Permitted Purposes**").

## 5. Processor Obligations

The Processor undertakes and warrants to the Controller that it shall:

### 5.1 Compliance with Law
5.1.1 Process personal data only on the documented instructions of the Controller, unless required to do so by UK or EU law to which the Processor is subject. In such a case, the Processor shall inform the Controller of that legal requirement before processing, unless that law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest.

5.1.2 Comply with all applicable obligations under UK GDPR, DPA 2018, and DUAA 2025 applicable to data processors.

### 5.2 Data Security
5.2.1 Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including as appropriate:
- Pseudonymisation and encryption of personal data
- Measures to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems
- Measures to restore availability and access to personal data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident
- A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organisational measures

5.2.2 The minimum security measures are set out in **Schedule 2 (Security Measures)**.

### 5.3 Staff Confidentiality
5.3.1 Ensure that all personnel authorised to process the personal data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

### 5.4 Data Subject Rights Assistance
5.4.1 Assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as possible, to fulfil the Controller's obligation to respond to requests for exercising data subject rights under Chapter III UK GDPR, including:
- Right of access (Article 15 UK GDPR)
- Right to rectification (Article 16 UK GDPR)
- Right to erasure (Article 17 UK GDPR)
- Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 UK GDPR)
- Right to data portability (Article 20 UK GDPR)
- Right to object (Article 21 UK GDPR)
- Rights relating to automated decision-making (Article 22 UK GDPR)
- DSAR requests including reasonable search and stop-the-clock provisions under DUAA 2025

5.4.2 The Processor shall:
- Notify the Controller immediately (within [[DSAR_NOTIFICATION_HOURS]] hours) upon receiving any request from a data subject
- Not respond to any data subject request directly unless authorised in writing by the Controller
- Provide the Controller with full cooperation and assistance in responding to any data subject request within [[DSAR_ASSISTANCE_TIMEFRAME]] days

### 5.5 Personal Data Breach Notification
5.5.1 Notify the Controller without undue delay (and in any event within [[BREACH_NOTIFICATION_HOURS]] hours) upon becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach.

5.5.2 The notification shall include at least:
- A description of the nature of the breach (including categories and approximate numbers of data subjects and records concerned)
- The name and contact details of the Processor's data protection officer or other contact point
- A description of the likely consequences of the breach
- A description of the measures taken or proposed to address the breach, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects

5.5.3 The Processor shall cooperate fully with the Controller in investigating and documenting the breach and shall not take any steps to notify affected data subjects, the ICO, or any other regulator, unless expressly directed or authorised by the Controller in writing.

5.5.4 The Processor shall maintain a written log of all personal data breaches in accordance with Article 33(5) UK GDPR.

### 5.6 Record of Processing Activities
5.6.1 Maintain all records required under Article 30 UK GDPR in writing, including all categories of processing activities carried out on behalf of the Controller.

5.6.2 Make such records available to the Controller upon request.

### 5.7 Data Protection Impact Assessment
5.7.1 Provide reasonable assistance to the Controller with any data protection impact assessment (DPIA) or prior consultation with the ICO that the Controller reasonably considers necessary under Articles 35–36 UK GDPR and DUAA 2025 RLI registration obligations.

### 5.8 Deletion and Return of Data
5.8.1 At the choice of the Controller, delete or return all personal data (including all copies) to the Controller after the end of the provision of services, unless retention is required by applicable UK or EU law.

5.8.2 The Processor shall delete existing copies within [[DELETION_TIMEFRAME]] days of the Controller's instruction unless applicable law requires storage.

5.8.3 The Processor shall provide written certification to the Controller that it has complied with its deletion/return obligations.

### 5.9 Audit Rights
5.9.1 Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this Agreement and UK GDPR obligations.

5.9.2 Allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller.

5.9.3 **Audit process:**
- The Controller shall provide [[AUDIT_NOTICE_PERIOD]] days' written notice of any audit
- Audits shall be conducted no more than [[AUDIT_FREQUENCY]] per calendar year unless a Personal Data Breach has occurred or the ICO has directed an investigation
- Audits shall be conducted during normal business hours and shall not unreasonably interfere with the Processor's business operations
- The Controller shall bear its own audit costs unless the audit reveals a material breach by the Processor, in which case the Processor shall bear the reasonable costs of the audit
- Any information obtained during an audit shall be treated as confidential by the Controller

5.9.4 Alternatively, the Processor may satisfy its audit obligations under this clause by providing a current (issued within [[AUDIT_CERTIFICATE_MONTHS]] months) **ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, or equivalent certification** accompanied by a written statement from the Processor confirming that the security measures certified remain in place and effective.

## 6. Sub-processing

### 6.1 Authorisation
6.1.1 The Controller provides **[[SUB_PROCESSOR_AUTHORISATION_TYPE]]** (general / specific) authorisation for the Processor to engage sub-processors.

6.1.2 The Processor has the Controller's general authorisation to engage sub-processors from the list set out in **Schedule 3 (Sub-processors)** .

### 6.2 Notification and Objection
6.2.1 Where general authorisation is given, the Processor shall:
- Notify the Controller in writing of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of sub-processors at least [[SUB_PROCESSOR_NOTICE_DAYS]] days in advance
- Provide the Controller with sufficient information to enable the Controller to assess the proposed sub-processor's data protection compliance

6.2.2 If the Controller objects to a proposed sub-processor on reasonable data protection grounds within [[SUB_PROCESSOR_OBJECTION_DAYS]] days of receipt of notice, the Processor shall not engage that sub-processor and shall propose an alternative or the Controller may terminate the Service Agreement without penalty.

### 6.3 Sub-processor Obligations
6.3.1 The Processor shall impose on all sub-processors the same data protection obligations as set out in this Agreement (in particular, sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organisational measures).

6.3.2 The Processor shall remain fully liable to the Controller for the performance of any sub-processor's obligations.

6.3.3 A copy of the sub-processor agreement shall be provided to the Controller upon request.

## 7. International Transfers

7.1 The Processor shall not transfer any personal data outside the United Kingdom without the prior written consent of the Controller.

7.2 Where transfer outside the UK is necessary for the Permitted Purposes, the Processor shall ensure that an appropriate transfer mechanism under UK GDPR / DPA 2018 / DUAA 2025 is in place:

| Transfer Mechanism | Application |
|--------------------|-------------|
| **UK adequacy regulations** (Section 17A DPA 2018) | Where the destination country benefits from a UK adequacy decision |
| **UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA)** | For transfers to countries without adequacy decisions |
| **Addendum to EU SCCs** (ICO approved) | Alternative to IDTA |
| **Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)** | Intra-group transfers approved by ICO |
| **UK–US Data Bridge / other bespoke arrangements** | Where applicable |

7.3 The Processor shall provide the Controller with a completed Transfer Risk Assessment (TRA) for each restricted transfer.

7.4 Where the Processor engages a sub-processor outside the UK, the Processor shall ensure the same transfer protections apply.

7.5 The specific transfers (if any) are set out in **Schedule 4 (International Transfers)** .

## 8. Liability and Indemnification

8.1 Each Party shall be liable for any breach of UK GDPR, DPA 2018, or DUAA 2025 arising from its own failure to comply with its obligations under this Agreement.

8.2 The Processor shall indemnify the Controller against all claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including legal fees) arising from:
- Any breach of this Agreement by the Processor
- Any processing carried out by the Processor outside the Controller's documented instructions
- Any act or omission of a sub-processor of the Processor

8.3 The Controller shall indemnify the Processor against all claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including legal fees) arising from any breach of this Agreement by the Controller or any instructions given by the Controller that contravene UK GDPR.

8.4 Nothing in this Agreement shall limit or exclude any Party's liability under UK GDPR for:
- Administrative fines imposed by the ICO
- Claims by data subjects for damage under Article 82 UK GDPR
- Criminal liability under DPA 2018 / DUAA 2025

8.5 Total aggregate liability under this Agreement (excluding liability under clauses 8.4 and 8.2) shall be capped at [[LIABILITY_CAP_AMOUNT]].
