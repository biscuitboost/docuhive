## 8. Data Subject Rights

Under UK GDPR, DPA 2018, and DUAA 2025, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

### 8.1 Your Rights

| Right | Description | Timeframe |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Right to be informed** (Art 13/14) | Receive clear information about how your data is processed | Provided in this notice |
| **Right of access / DSAR** (Art 15) | Obtain confirmation of processing and access to your personal data | 1 calendar month (standard); can be extended under DUAA 2025 |
| **Right to rectification** (Art 16) | Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data | 1 calendar month |
| **Right to erasure** (Art 17) | Request deletion of your data where there is no compelling reason for continued processing | Without undue delay |
| **Right to restriction** (Art 18) | Restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances | Without undue delay |
| **Right to data portability** (Art 20) | Receive your data in a structured, commonly used format | 1 calendar month |
| **Right to object** (Art 21) | Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing | Upon request |
| **Rights re: automated decisions** (Art 22) | Not be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effects | Ongoing |
| **Right to explanation** (DUAA 2025 s.13-15) | Explanation of algorithmic/AI decisions affecting you | Reasonable timeframe |

### 8.2 Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) — DUAA 2025 Provisions

Under the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, the following provisions apply to DSARs made by staff members:

**Reasonable Search Obligation (DUAA 2025):** When responding to a DSAR, we are required to conduct a **reasonable and proportionate search** for the requested personal data. We will not be required to undertake disproportionate or speculative searches. Factors considered include:
- The nature and scope of the request
- The likelihood of relevant data existing
- The cost and burden of the search
- The availability of alternative means of retrieval

**Stop-the-Clock Provision (DUAA 2025):** Where a DSAR is complex or requires clarification, we may **pause the statutory response period** once to seek clarification or reasonable requests from you. The clock will stop from the date we request clarification until the date we receive your response. We will notify you in writing if we invoke this provision.

**Manifestly Unfounded or Excessive Requests:** Where a DSAR is manifestly unfounded or excessive, we may:
- Charge a reasonable fee (considering administrative costs); or
- Refuse to act on the request.

We will notify you of our decision and your right to complain to the ICO.
