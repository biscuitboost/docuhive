### 10.2 Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) — DUAA 2025 Provisions

The Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 introduced specific provisions relating to DSARs:

**Reasonable Search Obligation (DUAA 2025):** When responding to a DSAR, we are required to conduct a reasonable and proportionate search for the requested personal data. We will not be required to undertake disproportionate, speculative, or overly burdensome searches. Factors considered include the nature and scope of the request, the likelihood of relevant data existing, the cost and burden of the search, and the availability of alternative retrieval methods.

**Stop-the-Clock Provision (DUAA 2025):** Where a DSAR is complex or requires clarification, we may pause the statutory one-month response period once to seek clarification. The clock stops from the date we request clarification until the date we receive your response. We will notify you in writing if we invoke this provision.

**Manifestly Unfounded or Excessive Requests (DUAA 2025):** Where a DSAR is manifestly unfounded or excessive, we may charge a reasonable fee (based on administrative costs) or refuse to act on the request. We will inform you of our decision and your right to complain to the ICO.
