## 1. Disciplinary Procedure

### 1.1 Invitation to Disciplinary Meeting

**PRIVATE & CONFIDENTIAL**

[[employee_name]]
[[employee_address]]

[[date]]

Dear [[employee_name]],

**Re: Invitation to Disciplinary Meeting**

I am writing to invite you to a disciplinary meeting to discuss a matter that has been raised regarding your conduct/performance at work.

**Nature of Concern**

The purpose of this meeting is to discuss the following concern:

[[allegation_or_concern]]

**Supporting Documents**

Copies of the following documents are enclosed with this letter for your review:

[[supporting_documents]]

**Meeting Details**

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Date** | [[meeting_date]] |
| **Time** | [[meeting_time]] |
| **Location** | [[meeting_location]] |
| **Chair** | [[inviter_name]], [[inviter_role]] |

**Right to be Accompanied**

You have the statutory right to be accompanied at this meeting by a work colleague, a trade union representative, or a trade union official accredited by your employer. Please let me know by [[companion_notification_deadline]] if you intend to bring a companion and, if so, who that person will be.

If your chosen companion is unavailable at the scheduled time, we will endeavour to agree an alternative time for the meeting, provided it is reasonable and within five working days of the original date.

**Possible Outcomes**

At the conclusion of this meeting, a decision will be made based on the facts established. Possible outcomes include, but are not limited to:

- No further action (if the concern is not upheld)
- A written warning (if the matter is found to be misconduct or unsatisfactory performance)
- A final written warning
- **Dismissal** (if the matter is found to be gross misconduct)
- Other action short of dismissal (e.g., demotion, transfer, training referral)

All potential outcomes, including dismissal, will be considered only after a full and fair investigation and a balanced consideration of all evidence presented.

**Investigation**

The meeting follows an investigation that has already been conducted. If you have new evidence or witnesses you wish to call, please bring these to the meeting or notify me in advance.

**Further Information**

If you require any reasonable adjustments to participate fully in this meeting (including but not limited to language support, accessibility arrangements, or additional time), please contact me as soon as possible.

Please confirm your receipt of this letter and your attendance at the meeting by contacting [[inviter_name]] at your earliest convenience.

Yours sincerely,

[[inviter_name]]
[[inviter_role]]
[[company_name]]

### 1.3 Invitation to Appeal Meeting

**PRIVATE & CONFIDENTIAL**

[[employee_name]]
[[employee_address]]

[[date]]

Dear [[employee_name]],

**Re: Invitation to Appeal Meeting**

Thank you for your letter of appeal dated [[appeal_received_date]], in which you appealed the decision following the disciplinary meeting held on [[original_meeting_date]].

We have now arranged an appeal meeting to hear your appeal. The details are as follows:

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Date** | [[meeting_date]] |
| **Time** | [[meeting_time]] |
| **Location** | [[meeting_location]] |
| **Appeal Chair** | [[inviter_name]], [[inviter_role]] |
| **Grounds of appeal received** | [[grounds_of_appeal]] |

**Right to be Accompanied**

You continue to have the statutory right to be accompanied at this appeal meeting by a work colleague, a trade union representative, or a trade union official. Please inform me by [[companion_notification_deadline]] if you intend to bring a companion.

**Format of Meeting**

The appeal hearing will consider the grounds you have raised and will review the original decision. It is not a re-hearing of the original disciplinary case unless new evidence has come to light that could not reasonably have been made available at the original meeting.

**Possible Outcomes**

Following the appeal meeting, the appeal chair may:

- Uphold your appeal and overturn or vary the original decision
- Dismiss your appeal and confirm the original decision
- Refer the matter back for a new hearing with different decision-makers

The outcome of the appeal will be confirmed to you in writing, and that decision will be final (subject to any statutory employment tribunal claim you may wish to pursue).

**Documents**

Please bring to the meeting any additional evidence or written representations you wish the appeal chair to consider, and forward these to [[inviter_name]] at least [[document_submission_deadline_days]] working days before the meeting where possible.

Please confirm your receipt of this letter and your attendance at the meeting as soon as possible.

Yours sincerely,

[[inviter_name]]
[[inviter_role]]
[[company_name]]

### 2.4 Grievance Appeal Rights

**Right of Appeal**

If you are dissatisfied with this outcome, you have the right to appeal. Any appeal must:

- Be submitted in writing within **[[appeal_deadline_days]] working days** of receipt of this letter
- Clearly state the grounds on which you are appealing (e.g., procedural irregularity, failure to consider relevant evidence, disproportionate outcome, new evidence)
- Be addressed to: **[[appeal_contact]]** at **[[appeal_contact_address]]** or by email to **[[appeal_contact_email]]**

The appeal will be heard by a more senior manager who has not previously been involved in this matter. You will have the right to be accompanied at any appeal meeting.

If you do not appeal within the time limit specified above, the decision will be considered final, subject to any separate statutory rights you may have (including the right to present a claim to an employment tribunal).

**Employment Tribunal Time Limits**

Please note that if you wish to challenge this decision through an employment tribunal, separate statutory time limits apply. For most claims, a claim must be presented to an employment tribunal within **three months less one day** from the act or omission complained of (or, in the case of a grievance outcome, from the date the outcome decision was communicated to you). You are advised to seek independent legal advice or contact ACAS (www.acas.org.uk) regarding early conciliation if you are considering tribunal proceedings.

Yours sincerely,

[[decision_maker_name]]
[[decision_maker_role]]

## Statutory Boilerplate

**Data Protection:** The information contained in this letter and any associated documents is processed for the purposes of managing the employment relationship and in accordance with the Company's data protection policy and UK data protection law. Records of disciplinary and grievance proceedings will be retained in accordance with the Company's data retention schedule.

**Confidentiality:** This letter and its contents are confidential and intended solely for the named employee. If you are not the intended recipient, please notify the sender immediately and delete/destroy all copies.

**Equality Act 2010:** The Company is committed to ensuring that all individuals are treated fairly and that no employee is disadvantaged by any of the procedures set out in this letter on the grounds of a protected characteristic as defined by the Equality Act 2010.

**ACAS Code of Practice:** These procedures are designed to follow the ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures (2015). Failure to follow the Code does not of itself render the employer liable to proceedings, but employment tribunals may adjust any award by up to 25% for unreasonable failure to comply with the Code by either party.
