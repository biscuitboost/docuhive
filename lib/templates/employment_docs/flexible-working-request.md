## Important Information

Under the **Flexible Working Act 2023** (effective 6 April 2024), all employees have the **day one right** to request flexible working from the first day of their employment. Employees may make **two statutory requests** in any 12-month period. The employer must deal with requests in a **reasonable manner** and must notify the employee of the outcome within **two months** of receipt of the request (unless an extension is agreed).

The **eight statutory grounds for refusal** are set out in section 80G(1)(b) of the Employment Rights Act 1996 and are reproduced in Part B of this form.

## Statutory Boilerplate

**Data Protection:** The information provided in this form will be processed in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 for the purposes of considering the flexible working request and, where granted, administering the new working pattern. This record will be retained for the duration of employment plus [[retention_period_post_employment]] months following termination.

**National Minimum Wage / National Living Wage:** Any change to working hours or pattern must not result in the employee being paid below the applicable National Minimum Wage or National Living Wage rates. It is the employer's responsibility to ensure compliance.

**Equality Act 2010:** The refusal of a flexible working request should not be based on a protected characteristic under the Equality Act 2010. If an employee's request relates to a disability or caring responsibility, the employer should consider whether a reasonable adjustment duty or a right to time off applies separately.

**Right to Surrender:** An employee whose request has been granted is not required to continue working the new pattern indefinitely. The employee may request a return to their original (or another) working pattern by making a new statutory request, subject to the two-request-per-12-month limit. An employer may also propose a variation by mutual agreement.

**Variation of Contract:** Where a flexible working request is granted, the employee's terms and conditions of employment will be varied accordingly. A formal variation of contract letter will be issued separately where applicable.

**ACAS Code of Practice:** Employers should handle flexible working requests in accordance with the ACAS Statutory Code of Practice on Handling Flexible Working Requests. Failure to follow the Code does not of itself render the employer liable to proceedings, but employment tribunals may take the Code into account when considering relevant complaints.
