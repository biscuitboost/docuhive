## 3. STATUS OF THE FREELANCER

### 3.1 Independent Contractor
The Freelancer is engaged as an independent contractor and **not** as an employee, worker, or agent of the Client. Nothing in this Agreement shall create a relationship of employment, partnership, or joint venture between the Parties.

### 3.2 No Employee Benefits
The Freelancer is not entitled to any employee benefits provided by the Client, including but not limited to: holiday pay, sick pay, pension contributions, parental leave, redundancy pay, bonus, private medical insurance, or any other benefits associated with employment.

### 3.3 IR35 Status
The Freelancer acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) The Client does not supervise, direct, or control the manner in which the Freelancer performs the Services;

(b) The Freelancer has the right to substitute a suitably qualified third party to perform the Services, subject to the Client's right to reasonably reject a proposed substitute where the Client has legitimate concerns about the substitute's suitability, qualifications, or security clearance;

(c) The Freelancer is not required to perform the Services personally and shall bear the cost of any substitute engaged;

(d) The Freelancer is free to work for other clients during the Term;

(e) The Freelancer is not subject to the Client's disciplinary or grievance procedures;

(f) The Freelancer bears the financial risk of the engagement, including the risk of non-payment by the Client;

(g) The Freelancer shall be responsible for their own tax and National Insurance contributions;

(h) The Freelancer may decide the hours, timing, and location of work, subject to any reasonable deadlines agreed with the Client.

### 3.4 Taxation
The Freelancer is solely responsible for all tax liabilities, National Insurance contributions, VAT (where applicable), and any other statutory payments arising from this engagement. The Freelancer shall indemnify the Client against any claims, penalties, or interest arising from the Freelancer's failure to make such payments.

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 5.1 IP Assignment
[[ip_assignment]] (If **true**): All Intellectual Property Rights in the Deliverables shall automatically vest in and be assigned to the Client upon full payment of the Fee. The Freelancer hereby assigns to the Client, with full title guarantee and by way of present and future assignment, all Intellectual Property Rights in the Deliverables throughout the world for the full term of those rights.

### 5.2 Pre-Existing Materials
For the avoidance of doubt, any Intellectual Property Rights in the Freelancer's pre-existing materials, tools, methodologies, and software (the **"Pre-Existing IP"**) shall remain vested in the Freelancer. Where the Freelancer incorporates Pre-Existing IP into a Deliverable, the Freelancer grants the Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide licence to use such Pre-Existing IP as part of the Deliverable for the Client's business purposes.

### 5.3 Moral Rights
To the extent permitted by law, the Freelancer waives all moral rights under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 in relation to the Deliverables.

### 5.4 Further Assurance
The Freelancer shall execute all documents and do all acts reasonably requested by the Client to perfect, register, or record the Client's ownership of the Intellectual Property Rights assigned under this clause.

### 5.5 Definition
**"Intellectual Property Rights"** means patents, rights in inventions, copyright and related rights, moral rights, trademarks and service marks, business names and domain names, rights in get-up, rights in goodwill or to sue for passing off, rights in designs, rights in computer software, database rights, rights in confidential information (including know-how), and any other intellectual property rights, in each case whether registered or unregistered and including all applications (or rights to apply) for, and renewals or extensions of, such rights, and all similar or equivalent rights or forms of protection which subsist or will subsist now or in the future in any part of the world.

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 6.1 Confidential Information
The Freelancer acknowledges that during the Term they may have access to or be entrusted with Confidential Information belonging to the Client or its clients, contractors, or suppliers.

### 6.2 Obligation
The Freelancer shall:

(a) Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the Client's prior written consent;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the Services;

(d) Take all reasonable steps to prevent unauthorised access, disclosure, or use of Confidential Information.

### 6.3 Exclusions
The obligations in clause 6.2 shall not apply to information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Freelancer;

(b) Was lawfully in the Freelancer's possession prior to disclosure;

(c) Is received from a third party not under a duty of confidentiality;

(d) Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority (provided the Freelancer notifies the Client promptly where legally permitted).

### 6.4 Return of Information
Upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, or upon the Client's request, the Freelancer shall promptly return or destroy (at the Client's option) all Confidential Information and copies thereof, and certify in writing that this has been done.

### 6.5 Duration
The obligations in this clause shall survive termination or expiry of this Agreement and continue for a period of [[confidentiality_period_years]] years.

## 7. DATA PROTECTION

### 7.1 Compliance
Each Party shall comply with its obligations under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

### 7.2 Personal Data
Where the Freelancer processes Personal Data on behalf of the Client in the course of performing the Services, the Freelancer shall:

(a) Only process Personal Data in accordance with the Client's documented instructions;

(b) Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect Personal Data;

(c) Not transfer Personal Data outside the UK without the Client's prior written consent and appropriate safeguards;

(d) Promptly notify the Client of any Personal Data breach;

(e) Assist the Client in complying with its data protection obligations;

(f) Delete or return all Personal Data at the end of the Services.

### 7.3 Data Processing Agreement
The Parties shall enter into a separate Data Processing Agreement where required by applicable data protection law.

## 8. TERMINATION

### 8.1 Notice
Either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving [[termination_notice_days]] days' written notice to the other Party.

### 8.2 Immediate Termination
Either Party may terminate this Agreement with immediate effect by written notice if:

(a) The other Party commits a material breach of this Agreement which, if capable of remedy, is not remedied within [[cure_period_days]] days of written notice;

(b) The other Party becomes insolvent, enters administration, has a receiver appointed over its assets, passes a resolution for winding up, or is dissolved;

(c) The Freelancer is, in the Client's reasonable opinion, unable to perform the Services due to illness, incapacity, or unavailability for a continuous period of [[incapacity_period_days]] days.

### 8.3 Consequences of Termination
Upon termination or expiry:

(a) The Client shall pay the Freelancer for all Services properly performed up to the date of termination;

(b) Each Party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information and materials;

(c) Clauses which expressly or by implication survive termination shall remain in full force (including clauses 5 (Intellectual Property), 6 (Confidentiality), 7 (Data Protection), 9 (Limitation of Liability), and 10 (Governing Law)).

### 8.4 Accrued Rights
Termination shall not affect any accrued rights or remedies of either Party.

## 9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 9.1 No Exclusion
Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits liability for: (a) death or personal injury caused by negligence; (b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation; (c) any other liability that cannot be excluded or limited by law.

### 9.2 Financial Cap
Subject to clause 9.1, the total liability of either Party to the other arising from or in connection with this Agreement (whether in contract, tort, negligence, breach of statutory duty, or otherwise) shall be limited to [[liability_cap_amount]] times the total Fee payable under this Agreement.

### 9.3 Excluded Losses
Subject to clause 9.1, neither Party shall be liable to the other for any: (a) loss of profits; (b) loss of business or business opportunity; (c) loss of revenue; (d) loss of anticipated savings; (e) loss of goodwill or reputation; (f) loss of or corruption of data; or (g) indirect or consequential loss or damage.

### 9.4 Proportionate Liability
Where the Freelancer's liability arises from a breach caused by a substitute or subcontractor engaged by the Freelancer, the Freelancer's liability shall not exceed that as determined under this clause 9.

## 12. BOILERPLATE

### 12.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and representations (whether written or oral) relating to its subject matter. Each Party acknowledges that they have not relied on any representation not expressly set out in this Agreement.

### 12.2 Variation
No variation of this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

### 12.3 Waiver
No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver, nor shall any single or partial exercise preclude any further exercise of that right.

### 12.4 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, that provision shall be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, or if not possible, severed, and the remainder of the Agreement shall continue in full force.

### 12.5 Notices
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered personally, by recorded delivery post, or by email to the addresses set out in clause 1. Notices are deemed received: (a) if delivered personally, at the time of delivery; (b) if sent by recorded post, 48 hours after posting; (c) if sent by email, at the time of transmission (unless the sender receives an automated non-delivery notification).

### 12.6 Assignment
The Freelancer may not assign, novate, subcontract, or otherwise transfer any of its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the Client's prior written consent. The Client may assign this Agreement to any affiliate or successor entity without the Freelancer's consent, provided that the assignee agrees to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.

### 12.7 Third Party Rights
A person who is not a Party to this Agreement shall have no right to enforce any term of this Agreement under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999.

### 12.8 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in any number of counterparts, each of which when executed shall constitute an original, and all counterparts together shall constitute one agreement.

### 12.9 No Partnership
Nothing in this Agreement is intended to, nor shall it, create a partnership, joint venture, or relationship of principal and agent between the Parties.

### 12.10 Force Majeure
Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement if such failure or delay is caused by circumstances beyond that Party's reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, fire, flood, pandemic, war, civil unrest, strike, lockout, or failure of public utilities. The affected Party shall notify the other Party promptly and use reasonable endeavours to mitigate the impact.
