## 2. BACKGROUND / RECITALS

(A) The Employee has been employed by the Employer pursuant to a contract of employment dated [[employment_contract_date]] (the **"Contract of Employment"**), most recently in the position of [[job_title]].

(B) The Employee's employment with the Employer will terminate on [[termination_date]] (the **"Termination Date"**).

(C) The Parties wish to settle all actual and potential claims (including any claims of which the Employee is not aware and that may arise in the future) arising out of or in connection with the Employee's employment and its termination on the terms set out in this Agreement.

(D) The Employee has received independent legal advice as required by section 203 of the Employment Rights Act 1996 and the ACAS Code of Practice on Settlement Agreements (Code of Practice 4).

(E) The Parties intend this Agreement to be a settlement agreement satisfying the conditions set out in section 203(3) of the Employment Rights Act 1996 and the relevant equivalent provisions in other legislation governing the waiver of claims.

## 4. TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT

### 4.1 Termination
The Employee's employment with the Employer will terminate by mutual agreement on the Termination Date. The Employee will continue to perform their duties and comply with all obligations under the Contract of Employment up to the Termination Date.

### 4.2 Garden Leave / PILON
From [[garden_leave_start_date]] until the Termination Date, the Employer may at its discretion: (a) require the Employee to remain at home and not attend work (garden leave); or (b) make a payment in lieu of notice (PILON) in accordance with the Contract of Employment. During any period of garden leave, the Employee shall comply with all post-termination restrictions and remain available to answer reasonable queries.

### 4.3 Return of Property
On or before the Termination Date, the Employee shall return to the Employer all property, documents, data, keys, devices, and any other materials belonging to the Employer or any Associated Company, and shall delete or destroy any copies held on personal devices or systems (certifying in writing that this has been done).

### 4.4 Accrued Rights
Termination shall not affect any rights or obligations that have accrued prior to termination.

## 5. PAYMENTS

The Employer shall make the following payments to the Employee (or, where appropriate, to HM Revenue & Customs or the relevant pension scheme):

### 5.1 Notice Pay / Payment in Lieu of Notice (PILON)
A payment of [[notice_pay_gross]] gross, representing [[notice_pay_description]]. This payment is subject to deductions for income tax and National Insurance contributions in the usual way.

### 5.2 Statutory Redundancy Pay
A payment of [[statutory_redundancy_pay]], being the statutory redundancy payment to which the Employee is entitled under sections 162–163 of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (if applicable). This payment is tax-free up to the statutory limit.

### 5.3 Compensatory Payment
A compensation payment of [[compensatory_payment_gross]] gross (the **"Compensatory Payment"**).

**Important:** The first £30,000 of the Compensatory Payment (or such part of the total termination payments as falls within the £30,000 exemption under sections 401–403 of the Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003) will be paid without deductions for income tax. Any amount of the Compensatory Payment exceeding £30,000 (when aggregated with other termination payments that are taxable but eligible for the £30,000 exemption) will be subject to income tax and National Insurance contributions in the usual way. The Employer reserves the right to calculate the precise allocation of the £30,000 exemption across the total termination package.

### 5.4 Holiday Pay
A payment of [[holiday_pay_outstanding_gross]] gross in respect of accrued but untaken holiday entitlement up to the Termination Date. This payment is subject to deductions for income tax and National Insurance contributions.

### 5.5 Tax Indemnity
The Employee shall indemnify the Employer and any Associated Company against any additional liability to income tax or National Insurance contributions (including interest and penalties) that arises in respect of any payment made under this clause 5, to the extent that such liability arises as a result of the Employee's act, omission, or provision of incorrect information.

### 5.6 Timing of Payments
Subject to clause 9 (Right to Rescind), the payments under this clause 5 shall be made on or before the Payment Date, provided that the Employee has signed this Agreement and the relevant solicitor's certificate (Schedule 2) has been provided, and the rescission period has expired without the Employee exercising their right to rescind.

## 9. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 9.1 Employee's Obligation
Subject to clause 9.2, the Employee shall keep the terms of this Agreement confidential and shall not disclose them to any person other than:

(a) The Employee's spouse, civil partner, or immediate family members (who must themselves keep the information confidential);

(b) The Employee's professional legal and financial advisers;

(c) HM Revenue & Customs or other government bodies as required by law;

(d) A court or tribunal in connection with legal proceedings to enforce this Agreement.

### 9.2 Permitted Disclosures (Protected Disclosures)
Nothing in this clause 9 (or any other provision of this Agreement) prevents the Employee from:

(a) Making a protected disclosure within the meaning of Part IVA of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (the Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998);

(b) Reporting any matter to a relevant regulator, supervisory authority, or law enforcement agency (including but not limited to the Equality and Human Rights Commission, the Health and Safety Executive, the Information Commissioner's Office, the Financial Conduct Authority, the Pensions Regulator, or HM Revenue & Customs);

(c) Participating in any investigation or proceeding conducted by any such regulator, authority, or agency;

(d) Disclosing information that is required to be disclosed by law or court order.

### 9.3 Employer's Obligation
The Employer shall keep the terms of this Agreement confidential save to the extent necessary to implement its terms, comply with legal or regulatory obligations, or obtain legal or accounting advice. The Employer may disclose the fact of the Employee's departure and the Termination Date to employees and third parties where reasonably necessary for business purposes.

### 9.4 Announcements
Neither Party shall make any public announcement or press release relating to this Agreement or the termination of the Employee's employment without the prior written consent of the other Party (such consent not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed).

## 10. NON-DEROGATORY STATEMENTS

### 10.1 Mutual Undertaking
Each Party undertakes that they shall not make, publish, or communicate any derogatory, defamatory, or disparaging statements (whether orally, in writing, electronically, or via social media) concerning the other Party, the other Party's officers, employees, agents, products, services, or business affairs.

### 10.2 Permitted Statements
Nothing in this clause prevents either Party from providing truthful information in the course of legal proceedings, regulatory investigations, or as otherwise required by law.

## 11. WAIVER OF CLAIMS

### 11.1 Full and Final Settlement
The Employee agrees that the terms of this Agreement are in full and final settlement of all Claims (as defined in clause 3) arising out of or in connection with the Employee's employment with the Employer or any Associated Company, and the termination of that employment.

### 11.2 Statutory Claims Waived
Without limiting the generality of clause 11.1, the Employee waives all claims (including all past, present, and future claims) arising under the following legislation:

(a) Section 203 of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (unfair dismissal, written statement of particulars, guarantee payments, suspension from work on medical grounds, time off work, redundancy, insolvency, and other rights);

(b) The Equality Act 2010 (discrimination, harassment, and victimisation on grounds of age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage and civil partnership, pregnancy and maternity, race, religion or belief, sex, and sexual orientation);

(c) The Part-time Workers (Prevention of Less Favourable Treatment) Regulations 2000;

(d) The Fixed-term Employees (Prevention of Less Favourable Treatment) Regulations 2002;

(e) The Agency Workers Regulations 2010;

(f) The Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 2006 (TUPE);

(g) The Working Time Regulations 1998 (including holiday pay and rest breaks);

(h) The National Minimum Wage Act 1998 and the National Living Wage Regulations 2015;

(i) The Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992;

(j) The Protection from Harassment Act 1997;

(k) The Data Protection Act 2018 and the UK General Data Protection Regulation;

(l) Section 44 of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (health and safety detriment);

(m) Section 47B of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (detriment for making protected disclosures);

(n) Sections 47C–47G of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (family-friendly statutory rights);

(o) Section 48 of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (detriment in relation to working time and other rights);

(p) The Employment Act 2008 (deductions from wages);

(q) The Apprenticeships, Skills, Children and Learning Act 2009;

(r) The Pensions Act 2008 (auto-enrolment);

(s) The Re-employment of Teachers etc. Regulations 1997;

(t) Any claim for breach of contract (including the Contract of Employment and any implied terms, including the implied term of mutual trust and confidence);

(u) Any claim for wrongful dismissal, notice pay, or payment in lieu of notice;

(v) Any claim for damages for injury to feelings, personal injury, or psychiatric injury (whether known or unknown at the date of this Agreement);

(w) Any claim in tort (including negligence, nuisance, and defamation);

(x) Any claim for an unlawful deduction from wages;

(y) Any claim under the employer's pension scheme (other than accrued rights which are preserved);

(z) Any claim for harassment, bullying, or victimisation.

### 11.3 Unknown Claims
The Employee acknowledges that they may have claims of which they are not aware and which may arise in the future. The Employee agrees that this Agreement extends to all such unknown and future claims and that they are settling them voluntarily with the benefit of independent legal advice.

### 11.4 Excluded Claims
Notwithstanding the foregoing, the following claims are not waived:

(a) Claims in respect of accrued pension rights under an occupational pension scheme (other than those waived under this Agreement);

(b) Claims arising after the date of this Agreement that cannot lawfully be waived;

(c) Any claim for personal injury of which the Employee is not aware and could not reasonably be expected to be aware at the date of this Agreement (the Employee acknowledges that no personal injury claims are currently known and that they have not suffered any accident or injury arising out of or in the course of their employment);

(d) Claims under the Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998 or under Part IVA of the Employment Rights Act 1996 in respect of protected disclosures made after the date of this Agreement; and

(e) Any other claim that cannot be excluded or restricted by law.

### 11.5 Conditions for Validity
The Employee acknowledges that the conditions set out in section 203(3) of the Employment Rights Act 1996 (and the equivalent provisions in other relevant legislation) must be satisfied for this Agreement to be valid as a settlement agreement. Those conditions include:

(a) The Agreement is in writing;

(b) The Agreement relates to the particular proceedings or complaint;

(c) The Employee has received independent legal advice from a qualified lawyer as to the terms and effect of the Agreement and its application to the claims which are intended to be waived;

(d) The adviser has a current practising certificate and is identified in the Agreement;

(e) The adviser has relevant and current indemnity insurance;

(f) The Agreement states that the statutory conditions regulating settlement agreements under the relevant legislation are satisfied.

### 14.2 Notice of Rescission
Notice of rescission must be given in writing to:

[[employer_notice_address]]

and must be received by the Employer within the rescission period.

### 14.3 Effect of Rescission
If the Employee exercises the right to rescind, this Agreement shall be void and of no effect, and:

(a) No payments under clause 5 shall be made (other than any sums which are otherwise due under the Contract of Employment or by statute);

(b) The termination of employment shall proceed on the Termination Date in accordance with the Contract of Employment (or as otherwise agreed);

(c) The Employee shall repay to the Employer any sums paid under this Agreement within 14 days of exercising the right to rescind.

### 14.4 Irrevocability After Rescission Period
If the Employee does not exercise the right to rescind within the rescission period, this Agreement shall become binding and irrevocable.

## 15. BOILERPLATE

### 15.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement (together with the Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and representations (whether written or oral) relating to its subject matter, except that this Agreement does not supersede any provisions of the Contract of Employment which are expressed to survive termination.

### 15.2 Variation
No variation of this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

### 15.3 Waiver
No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this Agreement shall operate as a waiver, nor shall any single or partial exercise preclude any further exercise of that right.

### 15.4 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, that provision shall be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, or if not possible, severed, and the remainder of the Agreement shall continue in full force.

### 15.5 Notices
All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered personally, by recorded delivery post, or by email. Notices are deemed received: (a) if delivered personally, at the time of delivery; (b) if sent by recorded post, 48 hours after posting; (c) if sent by email, at the time of transmission (unless the sender receives an automated non-delivery notification).

### 15.6 Assignment
Neither Party may assign, novate, or otherwise transfer any of its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that the Employer may assign this Agreement to any purchaser of its business or assets or to any Associated Company.

### 15.7 Third Party Rights
A person who is not a Party to this Agreement shall have no right to enforce any term of this Agreement under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999, except that the Associated Companies may enforce clauses 9 (Confidentiality), 10 (Non-Derogatory Statements), and 11 (Waiver of Claims).

### 15.8 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in any number of counterparts, each of which when executed shall constitute an original, and all counterparts together shall constitute one agreement.

### 15.9 Further Assurance
The Employee shall, at the Employer's reasonable request, execute any documents and do all acts reasonably required to give effect to the terms of this Agreement.
